B. Commercial machinery we dropped

This is what the live site does to monetise a small, chartered inventory. Most of it is missing from the redesign — grouped by screen, worst first.

What the redesign genuinely improves — keep it.
  • One search box instead of three. Live has separate search widgets per page with different fields; ours is one persistent bar.
  • Flexible dates + the price strip around the chosen dates. Live has nothing like it — it only hints at a cheaper period in one line of text. This is the single best answer to thin inventory.

  • Legible price hierarchy: one price, one discount pill, one “what’s included” line, instead of the live page’s competing red/orange/green blocks.

C. Call centre vs e-commerce

How hard the phone is pushed today

  • 85 occurrences of 0241 999 on a single search-results page — one on every one of the 81 hotel cards, plus header, plus footer.
  • An auto-firing overlay on the results page: “Nu te poți decide? Consultanții noștri sunt aici pentru tine. Sună acum” — it covers the header before the user has scrolled.
  • Header on every page: “Rezervați online sau la telefon” — the phone is presented as an equal-rank channel, first among equals.
  • Hotel page: the number sits in the middle column next to the description, plus a “Sună acum” item in the section tab bar.
  • Coverage: 10:00–18:00 only.

What you literally cannot do without calling

  • Instalments. “…menționează consultantului tău de turism că dorești să achiți în rate și vei primi un link special de plată”. The headline affordability product is phone-gated.
  • Holiday-card bookings. “…vei fi contactat de către un specialist de-al nostru”.
  • Gift vouchers. “Comanda ta va fi preluată în cel mai scurt timp de unul dintre consultanții noștri”.
  • Cancellations. Message via contact form or Facebook; “fiecare solicitare … echipa noastră de consultanți analizează”.
  • Storno insurance. Sold on request, with a hard deadline most buyers never hear about.
Why this caps the business. The phone is a fixed-capacity channel priced per conversation, so growth costs headcount — and it is exactly the resource that is scarcest right now, mid-season, after the July departures. It is open 10:00–18:00 while a meaningful share of leisure shopping happens in the evening; those users hit a number that nobody answers, and the site offers them no self-service alternative for instalments, vouchers or a cancellation. It also blinds measurement: any funnel instrumentation stops at “user saw a phone number”, which is precisely the visibility gap that already froze the Hotel Details rollout at 10%. And with the number repeated 85 times on one page, the site actively teaches its best-converting users to bypass the funnel we are trying to optimise.
Why the phone is nonetheless a genuine USP here — and must not be deleted. LITRO does not resell a global marketplace; it sells its own chartered allotment on one 245 km coast. That means (a) inventory runs out, and when it does, a human can move the guest to another hotel in the same allotment in one call — a website that says “no results” just loses the booking; (b) the product has real complexity that is hard to self-serve: sunbeds included or not, resort tax at reception, room allocation decided at check-in, child age bands that decide both price and admission; (c) a large part of the audience is families and older buyers paying several thousand Lei to a licensed agency — for them a human voice is the trust product, and the “licență din 2005 · 270 000 turiști” story only lands when someone picks up; (d) the phone rescues the transactions the site can’t yet do — vouchers, instalments, split payments. The correct move is not to remove the phone. It is to stop using it as the default and start using it as a scalpel.

And what disappears: the phone number on all 81 result cards, the auto-firing “Nu te poți decide?” overlay, and “Rezervați online sau la telefon” as the header’s primary message. The number stays in the header — once, quiet, with a live open/closed state.

How you know it worked. Instrument it before changing it, otherwise this is a religious argument: share of bookings completed without human contact; calls per 100 sessions (should fall) and conversion of the calls that remain (should rise, because they are now the hard cases); callback-slot fill rate outside opening hours; instalment and voucher bookings completed self-service; and the cost per booking split by channel. Given the GA4/Growthbook measurement problem that already froze the Hotel Details rollout at 10%, fixing measurement is a precondition for this whole shift, not a follow-up.
